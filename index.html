Liquid Propellant Rocket Engine & Test Stand

My multi-disciplinary capstone project was to design, fabricate, and test a blow-down feed system and a test article (rocket engine). It forced me to apply concepts from thermodynamics, fluids, and manufacturing. I also learned how to motivate, and manage a diverse team (2 ME, 1 CPE, 1 CS), coordinate with public safety to run a test campaign on campus, and train the next generation of students that will use the feed system.

Our test campaign concluded with a hard start (an explosion that extinguished the flame) but successfully set up the infrastructure for future students to compete in the Collegiate Propulsive Lander Challenge.

LifelinePi: Telehealth Television

LifelinePi was the startup I cofounded in the spring of 2020. After interviewing healthcare professionals and patients we identified three major barriers to telehealth for older adults: smart device availability, comfort with technology, and internet connectivity. I developed a business model and prototype that solved and removed the first two barriers by providing healthcare systems with a device that could be set up in older adults living rooms and did not depend on a complicated user interface.

Our first iteration consisted of a Raspberry Pi that could video call a doctor through a patient‚Äôs home television by stitching together existing video calling apps, automated sign-in, and an HDMI connection.
